## Ben Li's Raspberry Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are a classic snack that everyone loves. I personally prefer adding raspberries for extra flavor and texture, as well as a pop of red color.

### Ingredients

- Unsalted Butter (2 sticks, 2*113 g)
- Granulated Sugar (200 g)
- Brown Sugar (220 g)
- Vanilla Extract (5 g)
- Eggs (2)
- All Purpose Flour (360 g)
- Baking Soda (5 g)  
- Frozen Raspberry 
- Salt (5g)
- Nestle Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips (1 bag)

### Instructions

1. Put the butter out and let it soften for about 15 minutes or until you can easily make a hole with finger.
2. Mix the butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla extract together in a bowl until it becomes light and fluffy (Starting to look creamy).
3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
4. In a separate bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, and salt.
5. Mix two bowls together. Be especially careful, only mix with folding motion, and do not overmix.
6. Add the frozen raspberries and chocolate chips.
7. Preheat the oven to 350 F
8. Make the dough into small balls. You can also add some chocolate chips on top so that they look better. Also make the doughs like a tall cylinder cause it will make the middle thicker after baking. (If you have extra time, freeze the dough for better results)
9. Bake for 12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
10. ENJOY!
