## Sarn's Basil Pesto Salmon Pasta

Put your name or github username somewhere
-->
My favourite, go to meal - Basil Pesto Salmon Pasta

### Ingredients
- 2 6 oz Salmon fillets
- 8 oz Pasta
- 1/2 cup Basil Pesto 
- Extra Virgin Olive Oil
- 3 minced garlic cloves 
- Salt
- Black Pepper
- Water
- 1 Lemon 
- Butter


### Instructions
1. Pat the salmon fillets completely dry with a paper towel. This ensures a crisp sear rather than steaming. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.

2. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add a tablespoon of salt. Add the pasta and cook for 1 minute less than the package instructions (to keep it al dente).

3. Before draining, dip a mug into the pot to save some pasta water. Drain the rest.

4. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil shimmers, place the salmon in the pan.

5. Press down gently with a spatula for 10 seconds to ensure contact. Cook for 4–5 minutes until the bottom is golden and crisp.

6. Flip and cook for another 2–3 minutes until the center is just barely opaque. Remove the salmon to a plate and let it rest.

7. Once the salmon has rested for 2 minutes, use two forks to flake it into large, chunky pieces. Discard the skin if you prefer. Set the salmon aside.

8. In the same skillet (lower heat to medium-low), add another drizzle of oil if the pan is dry. Add the minced garlic and lemon zest. Sauté for only 30–45 seconds until fragrant. Do not let the garlic brown.

9. Turn the heat to its lowest setting. Add the cooked pasta, the pesto, and 1/4 cup of the reserved pasta water to the skillet. 

10. Add the flaked salmon and the tablespoon of butter back into the pan. Fold gently so you don't break the salmon chunks into mush. If it looks dry, add more pasta water 1 tablespoon at a time.

11. Remove the pan from the heat entirely. Squeeze half the lemon over the pasta and sprinkle the grated parmesan cheese on top. Serve immediately.
