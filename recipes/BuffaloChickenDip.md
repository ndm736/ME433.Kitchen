## Brendan's Buffalo Chicken Dip

My buffalo chicken dip was a hit at a Super Bowl party back in February. Multiple people asked me for the recipe, so I figured that I would share it here. 

#### Ingredients

*   3 large boneless chicken breasts (boiled & shredded)
*   8 ounces cream cheese (cubed)
*   1 cup of ranch dressing (homemade or store bought)
*   1 cup hot sauce (I use Frank’s RedHot Buffalo sauce. You can add more as needed)
*   1 teaspoon ground black pepper
*   1 teaspoon garlic powder
*   1/2 cup green onion (chopped)
*   1.5 cups mozzarella cheese (shredded, divided)
*   1.5 cups mild cheddar cheese (shredded, divided)

##### Instructions

1.   Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat. Add the chicken breasts to the pot and return to a boil. Once the water is boiling again, remove the pot from the heat and cover with a tight-fitting lid. Allow the chicken to poach, covered, for roughly 25 minutes. When fully cooked, remove the chicken from the pot and allow the chicken to rest until it is cool enough to handle.  
2.   Once cool, shred. I just did it with my hands. The chicken should be easy to peel & shred.
3.  Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F and spray a 9x9 inch baking pan with non-stick cooking spray (or approximate size, I used a 10x10 inch pan yesterday). Make sure that the baking pan is able to be broiled. Some class cookware can be baked, but will shatter if broiled.
4.   To a medium saucepot over medium-low heat, add the cubed cream cheese, ranch, hot sauce, black pepper and garlic powder. Whisk constantly until the cream cheese has dissolved into the ranch and hour sauce. Remove from the heat. 
5.   Add the cooked & shredded chicken, chopped green onion, 1 cup of the shredded mozerella cheese and 1 cup of the shredded cheddar cheese to the sauce pot. Mix well to combine. Transfer contents to the prepared baking pan and top with the remaining shredded mozzarella and cheddar cheese.
6.  Bake for approximately 20-30 minutes, or until the cheese has melted and the sides are starting to bubble.
7.  Set oven to BROIL. Allow the buffalo chicken dips to cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the top is golden brown. Remove it immediately.

I hope you enjoy! I typically serve/eat it with crackers & celery slices. Here is a link with more suggestions on what to serve/eat buffalo chicken dip with in case you're not a fan of those options: https://bradleysfinediner.com/fresh-meat/poultry/chicken/what-do-you-eat-with-buffalo-chicken-dip/
